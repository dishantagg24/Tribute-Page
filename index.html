Mahatma Gandhi

The man who taught world the lesson of non-violence

Gandhi, fourth from the left, walking with followers on the Salt March toward Dandi where they plan to break the English backed salt laws.

Here's a time line of Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - Born in Porbandar, Western India.
  • 1882 - Gandhi, only 13 years old, marries Kasturba Kapadia, who is also 13.
  • 1888 - Gandhi studies law in London.
  • 1893 - Gandhi takes a job with an Indian law firm in South Africa, where he is quickly exposed to the racial discrimination practiced there.
  • 1906 - A discriminatory law is passed in the Transvaal region of South Africa forcing all Indians to register with the provincial government or else face punishment.
  • 1919 - Gandhi becomes a leader in the Indian National Congress political party. He campaigns for swaraj, or “self-rule.”
  • 1930 - Gandhi leads tens of thousands of Indians on a 240-mile (385-kilometer) march to the sea to collect their own salt. The march is a protest against a British tax on salt.
  • 1932 - Under a new viceroy, Gandhi is imprisoned again. While in prison he fasts to protest the British decision to segregate the so-called Untouchables.
  • 1942 - Gandhi, who in 1934 had resigned as leader and member of the Indian National Congress, becomes politically active again early in World War II, demanding immediate independence as India’s price for aiding Britain in the war.
  • 1947 - India formally achieves independence from British rule. However, the partition of the subcontinent into India and Pakistan is a great disappointment to Gandhi, who has long worked for Hindu-Muslim unity.Again Gandhi turns to nonviolence, fasting until Delhi rioters pledge peace.
  • 1948 - While on his way to prayer in Delhi, Gandhi is killed by a young Hindu fanatic who has been angered by Gandhi’s efforts to reconcile Hindus and Muslims.
